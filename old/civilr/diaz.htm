Antonio Diaz

MEChA, Campus Activist, Dentist

Antonio Diaz was born in Mexico in 1946. In his teens, his family moved to El Paso, Texas, where he completed high school. After serving three years in the military and one year in Vietnam, Diaz moved to Seattle in 1970 in order to attend Seattle Central Community College. Later he transferred to the University of Washington.

In Seattle, Diaz quickly became active in the Chicano movement. He helped launch the Seattle Central Community College chapter of MEChA in 1970. He participated in the grape boycotts and farm workers’ struggle and organized social events for the Latino community.

Today, Diaz is the Program Coordinator for Veteran Affairs at Seattle Central Community College, where he helps veterans and people of color with the admissions process, financial aid, and education.

Antonio Diaz shared his memories of the Chicano Movement in an interview conducted by Edgar Flores on October 12, 2006.
