KDP and ACWA:
Filipino Radicalism in the 1970s and 1980s

The 1960’s and 1970’s was a time where Filipino American youth were being politicized by the civil rights struggle, anti-Vietnam war protests, and de-colonization movements happening around the third world. One organization that emerged out of these collective struggles that politicized Filipino American youth was the Union of Democratic Filipinos (KDP).

The KDP’s first chapter started in the bay area of California in 1973 and the Seattle’s Chapter formed in 1974. The KDP embraced a dual platform of supporting the National Democratic movement in the Philippines led by the Communist Party of the Philippines, as well as working for socialism in the United States.

The Seattle Chapter of the KDP had organizers working in the Local 37, university students involved in campus organizing at the University of Washington, members doing youth organizing through a youth drop-in center in Seattle’s International District, and people conducting education work around the human rights violations of the Martial Law regime of Ferdinand Marcos in the Philippines.
