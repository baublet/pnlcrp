Open Housing News Coverage, 1962-1964

[Home]   [History]   [Oral Histories]  [Papers and Documents] [Open Housing News Coverage]

The open housing citywide ordinance, placed on the March 10, 1964 citywide ballot, was contentious in Seattle. Different Seattle-area newspapers, depending on their sympathies and political proclivities, alternately supported, condemned, or pointedly ignored "Proposition 1." Articles collected here are from the two major dailies, the Seattle Times and Seattle Post-Intelligencer; ethnic newspapers like the Filipino Forum; community papers like the Central District's The Facts, the West Seattle Herald, and the Ballard News Tribune; the Washington State Labor News; and the University of Washington's The Daily. Click the titles to read the articles. Articles are organized chronologically.

Trevor Goodloe's research report, The 1964 Open Housing Election: How the Press Influenced the Campaign , provides an analysis of these documents.

Thanks to Trevor Goodloe and Michael Sculze-Oechtering Castañeda and for collecting and scanning these articles.

Editorial in the Seattle Post-Intelligencer from February 28, 1964, which refused to take a clear stance on the measure.
The Central District's The Facts urged readers to demonstrate for open housing, October 18, 1963.
The UW's March 3, 1964 paper profiled students campaigning for open housing.

An anti-open housing ad published in the two major Seattle dailies, March 9, 1964.


The Ballard News Tribune neglected to mention the ordinance at all, even in an article urging voters to vote in the election. March 4, 1964. The West Seattle Herald acted as a lively forum for debate on the measure. October 10, 1963.
Washington State Labor News neglected to mention the open housing campaign in the months before the election despite their focus on local politics, made evident here by Proposition 1’s omission from their election checklist. February 1964.

The Daily was initially reticent to support Proposition 1, but received so many student letters eager to discuss the issue that they printed them in a separate section of the paper. March 5, 1964.

The Filipino Forum did not mention the open housing directly during the entire campaign.
