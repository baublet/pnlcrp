Special Section

 Carlos Bulosan
An Activist Heart

[FBI files] [Oral Histories]  [Photos]  [Documents]

Filipino-American author Carlos Bulosan is best remembered for his powerful 1943 novel/memoir America is in the Heart. At the heart of that book and in the heart of the author was a deep commitment to social justice activism, to the CIO labor movement, to racial and ethnic equality, to Philippine independence, to radical politics in both the US and the Philippines.

Before he was an author, he was a worker. He worked in the fields, he worked in the canneries, he worked as a dishwasher, a house servant, and a dozen other difficult jobs. Before he was an author, he joined unions and radical causes. He worked with the Communist Party, but never joined. More importantly, he worked with the cannery and farm workers unions that represented Filipino American workers. When no one else would employ him in the red scare of the early 1950s, he found a home with Local 37 of the ILWU, editing the yearbook for the cannery workers local.

This special section explores the activist life of Carlos Bulosan and publishes for the first time the files that the FBI compiled during more than a decade of surveillance. Here you will also find some of his writings and the memories of those who knew him, including an oral history of Josephine Patrick, his loving companion. The section also serves as a gateway to the Bulosan Collections in the Labor Archives and Special Collections in the University of Washington Libraries and features contributions from the Filipino American National Historical Society, ILWU Region 37, Cindy Domingo, and others.

Trevor Griffey and Conor Casey have curated these pages.

  • FBI files: Trevor Griffey has acquired more than 500 pages of FBI Survellance files and shares them here, along with an introductory essay by Rick Baldoz.
  • Josephine Patrick Oral History: Bulosan's campanion in the last six years of his life, Josephine Patrick shares memories of the writer and discusses her own lifetime of activism in a 1998 interview with Cindy Domingo.

  • Photographs: from the Filipino American National Historical Society

  • Documents: We have a documents and publications including digital copy of the 1952 Local 37 Yearbook, a collector's item edited by the renowned Filipino American writer, Carlos Bulosan.
