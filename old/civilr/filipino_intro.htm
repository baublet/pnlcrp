Introduction about activism

 

 

This section of the Seattle Civil Rights and Labor History Project is dedicated to the activism of these three generations of Filipinos in the Seattle Area from 1930’s to 1980’s. The content in this section is organized into three subsections and contains photo collections, documents, newspaper articles, activist oral histories, and research reports:

  • Alaskan Cannery Unionism: This section traces the history of Filipino Cannery union organizing. Starting in 1933 when the first Filipino cannery union was launched to the 1980’s when Filipino were still fighting against the racial discrimination in hiring practices, living conditions, and pay. Also included in this section is a legal defense organization for Filipino cannery workers that emerged in 1973, the Alaska Cannery Worker Association (ACWA). This organization filed three civil suits against cannery companies for racist hiring practices and working conditions.
  • Union of Democratic Filipinos (KDP): This section on the Seattle Chapter of the KDP takes a comprehensive look at their organizing through documents the KDP produced to build awareness around the Martial Law regime in the Philippines, and well as local organizing in the Filipino community in Seattle and in the Alaskan canneries. After the murders of two KDP organizers Silme Domingo and Gene Viernes, members of the KDP organized the Committee for Justice for Domingo and Viernes (CJDV). A collection of CJDV newsletters is also included in this section.
  • Filipino Youth Association (FYA) and Filipino American National Historical Society (FANHS): This section looks at role of these two community organizations and the work of their founders Fred and Dorothy Cordova. The FYA was formed in the 1960s and the FANHS was from in the 1980s.

 

  • Video Oral Histories: short biographies and streaming video excerpts of interviews with Dorothy Cordova, Fred Cordova, David Della, Cindy Domingo, Lynn Domingo, Frank Irigon, and Velma Veloria.

  • Photographs: from the Filipino American National Historical Society

  • Documents: We have a documents and publications including digital copy of the 1952 Local 37 Yearbook, a collector's item edited by the renowned Filipino American writer, Carlos Bulosan.
