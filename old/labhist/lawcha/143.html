Representing Teachers: Labor and Liberalism in Bel Kaufman’s Up the Down Staircase

Daniel Gilbert
University of Illinois

Abstract

This paper situates the explosion of teachers' labor militancy within a broader cultural conversation about public educators’ role in building the so-called Great Society. The paper centers on the work of Bel Kaufman, who drew on her experience as a New York City teacher in her 1965 novel Up the Down Staircase, which formed the basis for the 1967 Hollywood film of the same name. Kaufman became a prominent ally of teachers unions around the country from the mid-1960s forward, and her book articulated experiences and perspectives that resonated with millions of other teachers. The paper draws on new archival research in Kaufman’s papers, exploring the ways in which her novel became a touchstone for broader public reckoning with the workplace politics of public education.
