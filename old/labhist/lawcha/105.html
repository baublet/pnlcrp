Chicanos and the Left in the Long Sixties

Marco Rosales1, Andrew Higgins1, Diana Johnson1, Matthew Garcia2
1University of California, Davis, 2Arizona State University

Abstract

‘Chicanos and the Left in the Long Sixties’ presents a history of interracial and transnational labor and social activism. Collectively, this new scholarship examines previously unstudied or understudied sites and subjects of Chicano protest. Moving away from a traditional focus on the UFW in California, Marco Antonio Rosales details the lasting impacts of the farm worker movement on Arizona’s Chicano community. Andrew Higgins explores the forgotten legacy of the socialist union organizer Anne Draper, in particular, Draper’s organization of monthly food caravans to the striking workers of Delano. And finally, Diana Johnson’s work focuses on the Pacific Northwest to show how a coalition of Chicana, Black, and Asian-American women successfully organized within labor and university spaces despite lacking a larger demographic presence.
