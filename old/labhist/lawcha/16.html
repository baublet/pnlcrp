Challenging the Culture of Empire: The Legacy of Dr. Dionicio Valdés in Labor and Chicana/o Historical Scholarship

Jose G. Moreno1, Luis H. Moreno2, Nora Salas3, Jerry Garcia1, Dionicio Valdés4
1Northern Arizona University, 2Bowling Green State University, 3Grand Valley State University, 4Michigan State University

Abstract

This roundtable panel will examine the legacy and importance of Chicana/o and Labor Historian Dr. Dionicio Valdés. In 1991, Valdés published Al Norte: Agricultural Workers in the Great Lakes Region, 1917-1970, a pioneering book on Mexican agricultural workers in the Midwestern U.S. It also challenged the Southwest-centric historical focus of Chicana/o Labor History and Chicana/o Studies. He later co-edited Voices of a New Chicana/o History (2000) with Refugio I. Rochín. He followed up with Barrios Nortenos: St. Paul and Midwestern Mexican Communities in the Twentieth Century (2000) and Mexicans in Minnesota (2005). Valdés then published Organized Agriculture and the Labor Movement before the UFW:  Puerto Rico, Hawaii, California (2011), the first scholarly book to examine comparative agricultural labor production, imperialism and the labor movement in major agricultural unionization drives prior to the appearance of the United Farmworkers Union (UFW). In addition, for nearly 40 years Valdés has published influential and original monographs, academic articles and book reviews. Each panelist is a scholar and an activist, and will reflect on at least one text and discuss how his scholarship has influenced the fields of Chicana/o Labor History and Chicana/o Studies, and the role of activism in labor scholarship. Valdés will offer final comments and remarks on his current historical research projects and publications. The panelists intend to submit their papers for publication in the LAWCHA journal.
