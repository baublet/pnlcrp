Authors Meets Critics: Michael Kazin, War Against War: The American Fight for Peace, 1914-1918 & Lynn Dumenil, The Second Line of Defense: American Women and World War I

James Gregory1, Michael Kazin2, Lynn Dumenil3, Nancy Bristow4, David Brundage5
1University of Washington, 2Georgetown, 3Occidental, 4University of Puget Sound, 5University of California, Santa Cruz

Abstract

Marking 100 years since the United States entered World War I, two important new books examine the war at home: Michael Kazin, War Against War: The American Fight for Peace, 1914-1918; Lynn Dumenil, The Second Line of Defense: American Women and World War I.

In this roundtable the authors will discuss their work with Nancy Bristow, author of two books on WWI (American Pandemic: The Lost Worlds of the 1918 Influenza Epidemic; Making Men Moral: Social Engineering During the Great War) and David Brundage, author of the book, Irish Nationalists in America: The Politics of Exile, 1798-1998, and articles about the 1916 Easter Rising.
