The Watsonville Canning Strike, 1985-87

Peter Shapiro
Jobs with Justice

Abstract

Thirty years ago, Watsonville California was the site of a successful 18 month strike by 1,000 frozen food workers, most of them Mexican immigrant women. They fought off an attempt to decertify their union, forced the plant owner into foreclosure, and settled with the new owner after a five-day wildcat. Besides revitalizing what had been a virtually moribund Teamster local, the strike was a serious bid for political power by Watsonville's emerging Mexican majority.
