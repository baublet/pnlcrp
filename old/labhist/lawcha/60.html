Who Built the Empire: Rethinking the History of Labor and the U.S. Empire

Julie Greene1, Reena Goldthree2, Kornel Chang3, Colleen Woods4, Moon-Ho Jung5
1University of Maryland, 2Dartmouth College, 3Rutgers-Newark, State University of New Jersey, 4University of Maryland at College Park, 5University of Washington

Abstract

Labor historians have been energetic participants in the 'global turn' which is reshaping the discipline of history, with particular interest in connecting their emphasis on workers and class to the history of empire. Months ago a special double issue of Labor: Studies in Working-Class History was published on the theme of "Empire and Labor," and an anthology, Making the Empire Work, recently edited by Jana Lipman and Daniel Bender, also explored the topic. In addition there have been a number of important monographs published in recent years on workers and empire. This roundtable brings together scholars from both the special issue of LABOR and the Bender/Lipman anthology to take stock of the field and raise questions and issues it might address in the future.
