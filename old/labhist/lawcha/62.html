Building social awareness through public event organizing: The annual Labor, Social and Environmental Justice Fair

Students and faculty at California State University, Dominguez Hills put on an annual Labor, Social and Environmental Justice Fair, led by the Labor Studies program and the Labor and Social Justice Club. Now in its ninth year, this event fosters awareness of economic, racial and gender struggles, and how the labor movement intersects and supports social justice. Students take an organizing and service learning class to spearhead the event, network with faculty and students to create a social justice fair with labor at its core. Fifty organizations, half of which are labor unions and workers centers, set up booths to interact and inform college students and the several hundred high school students we bring, about their rights and opportunities to get involved in social change. Workshops provide opportunities to create in-depth dialogue and participatory activities around social justice themes, and art and music broaden the scope of the event. Labor unions provide the majority of the funding for the annual event. This presentation provides an overview of the fair as well as an analysis of how the event impacts the organizers and participants.
