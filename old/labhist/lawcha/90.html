“Made in the USA?”: Resisting Sweatshops in the 1990s

Beth Robinson
Texas A&M-Corpus Christi

Abstract

My paper examines the 1990s, as a significant period of anti-sweatshop activism in the United States. During this decade activists fought many of the same challenges as in previous eras, but adapted to a new landscape, with an increasingly mobile and global sweatshop industry. As parts of a larger anti-globalization, pro-worker, and pro-environment movement taking place at the turn of the century, these activists forced the means of production back to the forefront.
