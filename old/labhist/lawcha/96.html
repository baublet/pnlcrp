Unions & Social Media

Veronique Emond-Sioufi
Simon Fraser University

Abstract

Recent shifts in socioeconomic discourse have been widely perceived as opening up potential opportunities in a widespread union renewal effort. Given the digital media expansion that has run concurrent with labour’s decline, it seems clear that any potential shift toward a more dynamic and influential union movement will not only require significant social and ideological changes, but an active role on the part of labour activists and academics in the understanding and use of social media. This presentation will provide an overview of the academic research that has been done on unions’ use of social media and will highlight the challenges faced by the labour movement in utilizing social media as a means of expanding their profile and perceived significance to those sectors that could benefit from a larger role for labour. Finally, this presentation will explore potential lessons for the labour movement that can be drawn from research on social movements’ use of social media.
