Abstract

This roundtable discussion will feature a historical context of worker education at residential womenâ€™s schools. From the Bryn Mawr Summer School for Women Workers to the four regional UALE summer schools, women workers have found their voice, a supportive community and a pathway from activists to leaders. For some women, these schools offer a launching point in their leadership trajectory. We will compare these schools and pay particular attention to any global connections as well as what might be done to further global solidarity and educational opportunities for women workers in a changing labor movement.
