Rural Resistance: An Immersive Archive

Molly Mac1, Cristien Storm2, Kate Boyd3
1The Alice, 2If You Don't They Will, 3Shoreline Community College

Abstract

This is a multi-media, inter-disciplinary, immersive workshop that theorizes and visibilizes the rural, working class activist labors of Northwest anti-fascist organizers, with special attention to intersections of age, gender, and race. For twenty years, Kate Boyd and Cristien Storm have researched and organized against white nationalism in the Pacific Northwest. During this moment of escalating white nationalist violence, Kate and Cristien have partnered with feminist video artist and curator Molly Mac to interview rural activists who were active in the 1980’s and 1990’s, in order to build an inter-generational, non-teleological bridge that transmits the political imaginaries and sensibilities of the past to those struggling to organize in the present. Workshop participants will be immersed in a “living archive” video exhibition that both documents past resistance strategies and also engages in the trajectory of future efforts. Participants will simultaneously engage with an interactive training on the depth and breadth of the white nationalist movement, anti-fascist rural organizing praxis, and get a chance to practice developing their own anti-racist responses, rooted in cultural organizing.
