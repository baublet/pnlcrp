Great Lakes by Northwest: New Perspectives on Labor Organizing and Social Movements

Josué Q. Estrada1, Sergio González2, Jillian Jacklin3, Mario Sifuentez4, Naomi R. Williams5, Marc Rodriguez6
1University of Washington, 2The University of Wisconsin, 3UW-Madison, 4University of California-Merced, 5SUNY-Brockport, 6Portland State University

Abstract

How profoundly has the working class shaped United States society? In this diverse roundtable, participants will discuss their recent scholarship on the history of labor and social activism across the United States.

Emphasizing daily life, these historians explore how ethnicity, gender, race, religion, and citizenship influenced the opportunities and limitations available for workers as well as the ways in which workers have changed the trajectory of American life. With projects that range from the late nineteenth until well into the twentieth century, each talk will provide a new window for understanding how workers have organized, protested, and made meaningful political change through their struggles, labors, and social engagement. Each scholar uses a broad array of sources and methods in their work in order to uncover stories of people whose lives need remembering.
