“Driven to Desperation:” Race, Navassa Island, and the Problem of Free Labor in the Nineteenth Century

Dennis Halpin
Virginia Tech, History Department

Abstract

My paper uses the 1889 uprising on Navassa to complicate our understandings of the transition from slave to “free labor” at the end of the nineteenth century. Throughout the 1880s and 1890s, the Navassa Phosphate Company lured African-Americans with promises of high wages, company housing, and benefits. The reality failed to live up to these lofty promises; in fact, The New York Times proclaimed that the company’s employees were in effect “slaves.” The events of 1889 focused national attention on the island, the company’s employment practices, and galvanized labor and civil rights activists.
