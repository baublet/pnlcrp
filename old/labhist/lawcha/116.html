Radicals and the Democratic Party: Independent Political Action or Bore From Within, 1876-2016

The question of whether to work outside or within the Democratic Party is one that has long faced working-class radicals. In the absence of a national labor party in U.S., the question has acted as a plague on working-class movements due to its divisiveness. While some radicals view the Democratic Party as where movements go to die, others see working within the party as part of the political reality of the two-party system. The papers in this panel evaluate the tactics of radicals working both within and independent of the Democratic Party from the 1880s to the present.
