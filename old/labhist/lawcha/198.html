Mass Incarceration and the Working Class

Heather Ann Thompson1, Donna Murch2, Kelly Lytle Hernandez3, Julie Greene4, Chelsea Nelson5
1University of Michigan, 2Rutgers University, 3University of California-Los Angeles, 4University of Maryland, 5Development Director, IBEW 46

Abstract

The politics of criminalization and mass incarceration have deep historical roots that are tied up inextricably with issues of labor and class. This panel will examine criminalization from the perspective of labor and working-class history. Topics explored will include the politics of prison labor, deindustrialization and the criminalization of youth, immigration and the criminalization of unlawful entry, and ongoing work by activists in and beyond the labor movement to support men and women with a criminal record, both during and after imprisonment.
