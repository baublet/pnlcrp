The $15 Minimum Wage Movement in SeaTac and Seattle: Origins and Impact

Michael McCann1, Jonathan Rosenblum2, Megan Brown3, Conor Casey1, Sejal Parikh4
1University of Washington, 2Labor activist, community organizer, book author, 3University of Washington, former SEIU activist, 4Working Washington

Abstract

In November, 2013, the small suburban city of SeaTac passed Proposition 1, authorizing a $15 minimum wage policy phased in over several years; after surviving a lawsuit filed by business groups, the policy went into effect in January, 2014. Six months later, the Seattle City Council approved a similar $15 minimum wage policy. The victories in the Puget Sound catalyzed a proliferating array of campaigns to raise the minimum wage in cities and states around the nation as well as at the national level. This panel will reflect on the lessons that can be learned from the struggles by a broad coalition of labor unions, community partners, and elected officials around a host of issues concerning low wage workersâ€™ rights in the years following the Occupy movement, both in Seattle and beyond. The roundtable will feature local activists and activist-scholars involved in the minimum wage campaigns.
