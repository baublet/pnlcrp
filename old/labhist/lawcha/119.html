Civic Labors: A Roundtable on Scholar Activism and Working-Class Studies

John McKerley1, Michael McCann2, Jennifer Sherer1, Daniel Atkinson3, Emily LB. Twarog4, Eric Fure-Slocum5
1University of Iowa Labor Center, 2University of Wasington, 3Independent Scholar, 4University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, 5St. Olaf College

Abstract

This session is a roundtable loosely based around Civic Labors: Scholar Activism and Working-Class Studies (Illinois, October 2016). Chaired by one of the book's editors, the session will feature a variety of scholar activists engaging the audience in a discussion of the opportunities and challenges of scholarly work focused on facilitating social change in coordination with non-academic partners. Participants will consider the role of various power relationships--particularly race and gender--in shaping the success of such work. Specific examples of topics include the Iowa Labor History Oral Project, a forty-year-old oral history project co-directed and financed by scholars, archivists, and the Iowa AFL-CIO; the Harry Bridges Center for Labor Studies, a collaborative effort at building labor-academic alliances founded in a relationship between the ILWU and the University of Washington; and the Regina V. Polk Women's Labor Leadership Conference, an ongoing, university based program for empowering women labor leaders named for a pioneering woman in the IBT.
