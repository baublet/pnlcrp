Delivering the Magic: Circuses, "Servants," Disney and the History of Performance Labor

Lisa Phillips1, Luke Mielke2, Andrea Ringer3, David Witwer4
1Indiana State University, 2Macalester College, 3University of Memphis, 4Penn State

Abstract

This panel considers the history of "performance labor" in three distinct settings and from the late 19th through the late 20th centuries in the U.S. Panelists examine the performance of "luxury" by Black men in the all-white context of the growing hotel industry in Minneapolis in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, the unionization of circus "performers" in the 20th century in their struggles with "Big Top" business, Ringling Brothers, etc., and at Disney as "cast members" perform "happiness" and "exotic" tourist destinations for their "audiences." David Witwer will both chair and comment (unless and/or until we can find an additional person to take on either chair or commenting duties).
