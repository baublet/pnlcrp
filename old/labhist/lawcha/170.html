Janitors on Stage - Using Workers Oral Histories in a Union Contract Campaign

Bill Shields1 and Olga Miranda2
1Labor and Community Studies, City College of San Francisco, 2President, SEIU Janitors' Local 87

Abstract

A lecture-demonstration on an oral history theater project and its role in a successful union contract campaign. Developed over a two-year period, "Janitors on Stage" brought together the Labor and Community Studies Department of City College of San Francisco, SEIU Local 87 and the Labor Archives and Research Center at San Francisco State. Presenters will describe the project, involve attendees in a read-along of the performance material and analyze the part played by the performance in the union's successful contract campaign. They will conclude by leading attendees in a discussion of the intersection of worker activism, public history and art.

Labor and Community Studies' (LBCS) "Work Tales" oral history theater program brings workers together to explore key issues of concern in their lives and in their unions or workers' centers. The Labor Archives trains participants in how to conduct worker-worker oral history interviews, which they then store. LBCS staff work with the participants to hone their material into a performance piece of interwoven monologues, songs and choreography. This material is then performed for union members and the broader community.

In the "Janitors on Stage" iteration of this collaborative model, the partners built on the long-standing educational relationship between LBCS and Local 87. Brought in to help the union in 2015 with strategic planning for its 2016 city-wide contract campaign, LBCS staff proposed to use its oral history theater template as part of the union's visibility outreach. Local 87's largely Latino, Chinese and Yemeni membership clean the city's skyscraper offices after everyone else has gone home for the night. Often unseen by the city, these workers found their hard-won union standard threatened by the greed of some of San Francisco's most recognized new high-tech companies.

In the culminatilon of their hard-fought campaign, Local 87's members found themselves onstage performing in four languages one week, marching through downtown office atria the next and reaching a deal with their employers the week after that. The workshop will describe and analyze each step of this ultimately successful collaboration.
