Writing History Using F.B.I. Files: From Document Acquisition to Interpretation

Trevor Griffey1 and Daniel Chard2
1N/A, 2University of Massachusetts

Abstract

This interactive workshop will provide attendees with two presentations before opening up for questions: First, Trevor Griffey will survey some of the ways to access U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation (F.B.I.) domestic intelligence and counterintelligence files. His presentation will identify major sources for already-declassified and already-digitized files available on the internet. And it will provide tips on how to submit Freedom of Information Act requests for the declassification of files held by presidential libraries, the National Archives, and the FBI itself. Second, Daniel Chard will provide a critical introduction to the historiography of F.B.I. domestic intelligence and counterintelligence. His presentation with help historians decode and contextualize declassified F.B.I. documents.
