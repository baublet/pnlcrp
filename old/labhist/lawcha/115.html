Scales and Circuits of Waterfront Struggle: Home, Docks, City, Nation, and Beyond

Matt Basso1, Dolores Janiewski2, Grace Millar3, Colin Davis4
1University of Utah, 2Victoria University, Wellington, 3Independent Scholar, 4University of Alabama, Birmingham

Abstract

This panel focuses on the waterfront as a locus of struggle and identity contestation, both overt and hidden. In so doing we consider some of the different scales in which these dynamics occurred. Grace Millar examines the private spaces of the home during the 1951 Waterfront Strike in New Zealand as a major, and often ignored, site of struggle. Concentrating on strikes in 1890 and 1913, Matt Basso explores the way specific waterfront work practices, and larger city and national labor ideologies, influenced the contest over settler identity in New Zealand and the U.S. Dolores Janiewski traces the formation of Harry Bridges particular brand of waterfront radicalism by looking at local, national, and transnational influences on his life.
