Bridging Mexico and Seattle: History of Seattle's Ship Scalers Union Mural

Gigi Peterson
State University of NY at Cortland

Abstract

Painted in 1945 by US-born Pablo O'Higgins for Seattle's Ship Scalers Union, "The Struggle Against Racial Discrimination" hangs in the University of Washington's Kane Hall and is a powerful depiction of labor solidarity across borders. Along with a visual tour of this mural, the author will explore its history, which crosses borders and decades. Created in a context of Mexican-US labor and leftwing ties, the mural "disappeared" for some years, and activists who linked it to Chicano struggles pressed the University of Washington for its recovery.
