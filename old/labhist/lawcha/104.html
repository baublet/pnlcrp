Abstract

This panel session uncovers the hidden history of Tejana interventions as community laborers and activists who facilitated the creation of clinics, defended and bolstered female property rights, and addressed racism alongside sexism through cultural productions. Performing unpaid and volunteer labor, Tejanas have a long legacy of struggling on behalf of their families and communities.
