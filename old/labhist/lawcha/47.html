Localizing Labor History: Case Studies of Commemoration and Sites of Memory

Mary Anne Trasciatti1, Robert Forrant2, Erik Loomis3, James Barrett4, Fraser Ottanelli5
1Hofstra University, 2University of Massachusetts Lowell, 3University of Rhode Island, 4University of Illinois, Urbana, 5University of South Florida

Abstract

There is widespread agreement that labor history can and should be taught outside the academy. One approach is to create situations in which people encounter labor’s past in the very places where it happened. To that end, the papers in this session will explore ideas and practices for site-specific labor history in relation to the 1911 Triangle Shirtwaist Factory Fire in Greenwich Village, New York; 1912 Bread and Roses Strike in Lawrence, Massachusetts; and various National Park locations. Presenters will consider how the rise of social history, the appeal of the arts, social media, and other factors have opened up possibilities for public, i.e. non-academic, labor education and commemoration, and discuss the potential of labor spaces for helping to regenerate interest in and support for contemporary workers’ movements.
