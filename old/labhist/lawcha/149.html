Navigating the Mazes of Modernization in Puerto Rico: Organized Labor, Techno-Politics, and Welfare Activism

Jorell Meléndez-Badillo1, Emma Amador2, Ileana M. Rodríguez-Silva3, Aimee Loiselle1
1University of Connecticut, 2Brown University, 3University of Washington

Abstract

This panel explores various interactions between organized labor, government officials, and activists in Puerto Rico and the United States since the New Deal’s early days to the establishment of the Commonwealth and the country’s rapid industrialization. The papers survey different governmental programs and institutions, such as public health infrastructures, the University of Puerto Rico, and the Social Security Act, to explore the tensions lived by local actors while navigating complex colonial, political landscapes. Moreover, it offers new approaches that situate Puerto Rico within a broader transnational setting while being attentive to the agency and friction of local and international actors.
