An Anti-Racketeering Purge: The AFL-CIO'S Response to the McClellan Committee, 1956-1959

David Witwer
Penn State

Abstract

This paper will draw on the internal records of the AFL-CIO, including its Ethical Practices Committee, to trace organized labor's response to revelations of racketeering in the labor movement that emerged in the mid-1950s. The scale of the response and the techniques adopted in this purge resembled the labor movement's previous effort to counter Red Scare era concerns about communist influence in unions. This paper will place the episode in the larger context of the federation's historical response to the issue of corruption and it will also explore the problems that emerged with this particular response.
