The New Global Working Class and the Fight for Labor Rights

Annelise Orleck1, Marcel Van Der Linden2, Shelton Stromquist3
1Dartmouth College, 2International Institute of Social History, Amsterdam, 3University of Iowa

Abstract

The global working class is changing dramatically in composition and location. As precarity grows and traditional unions face repression and decline in an increasingly globalized economy, workers are rising to these challenges and crafting novel and promising responses. Marcel van der Linden and Annelise Orleck will examine these trends in working-class recomposition and new forms of transnational working-class collective action against anti-worker right-wing and neoliberal political influences that have themselves assumed a new global character.
