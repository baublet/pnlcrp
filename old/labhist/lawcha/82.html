"Union Time: Fighting for Workers' Rights" (documentary film)

Matthew Barr1 and Michael Honey2
1University of North Carolina at Greensboro, 2University of Washington Tacoma

Abstract

"Union Time: Fighting for Workers' Rights" is a feature-length documentary that tells the story of one of the greatest union victories of the 21st century--the fight to organize Smithfield Foods' pork processing plant in Tar Heel, North Carolina. From 1993 to 2008, workers struggled against dangerous working conditions, intimidation, and low pay.

Starting in 1993 the United Food & Commercial Workers Union (UFCW) began an organizing campaign at the plant that was to span 16 years. Smithfield Foods fought the union campaign tooth-and-nail, with everything from union busters to armed company security guards with the power of arrest to a RICO lawsuit against the union, all to no avail.

The union early on aligned itself with the North Carolina NAACP and its leader the Reverend William Barber, as well as with Jobs With Justice, the United Church of Christ and many other progressive organizations. It also waged an "Air War" that made effective use of social media.

Victory was achieved in a December 2008 election, and in July 2009 UFCW Local 1208 was created. For the 5,500 workers at Smithfield conditions have vastly improved. In 2013 a second four-year contract was signed.

The key theme of "Union Time"--is that commmunity-based unionism can and did succeed in building a groundbreaking union in one of the most anti-labor states in the country.
