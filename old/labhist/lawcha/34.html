Organizing and Representing Contingent Faculty

Trevor Griffey1, Lori Blewett2, Larry Cushnie3, Miranda Merklein4, Richard P. Moore5
1N/A, 2The Evergreen State College, 3Seattle University, 4New Faculty Majority (prior affiliation), 5University of Washington

Abstract

This roundtable will provide a space to discuss the variety of issues related to the organizing of contingent faculty into labor unions, and the way that labor unions can change the relationship between tenure track and non-tenure track faculty. Rather than presenting the issues of contingent faculty as monolithic, the roundtable will identify the different kinds of institutional positions that contingent faculty occupy in contemporary higher education, and the different kinds of organizing strategies and collective bargaining issues that emerge from trying to develop solidarity across disciplinary and professional positions.

• Trevor Griffey, the roundtable moderator, will open the conversation by identifying the diversity of non-tenure track faculty experiences, and the complex roles that unions can play in both ameliorating and reinforcing inequality between faculty of different ranks. He will also moderate the question and answer period.

• Lori Blewett will discuss the organizing of non-tenure track and tenure track faculty into a single labor union at The Evergreen State College, and draw from the experience of The United Faculty of Evergreen (AFT-NEA) to identify benefits and challenges of developing solidarity across faculty rank.

• Larry Cushnie will draw from his experience with Faculty Forward (SEIU) at Seattle University to discuss the challenges of teaching at Catholic institutions that have a social justice mission but rely upon contingent faculty labor and refuse to recognize or bargain with faculty labor unions.

• Richard Moore will draw from his experience with AFT Local 6486 at the University of Washington to discuss the ways in which faculty labor unions are essential to providing meaningful shared governance and increased job security to faculty in English language learning programs and university extension programs.

• Miranda Merklein will draw from her experience with New Faculty Majority to critically reflect on the role that non-profit organizations can play in raising consciousness, building power, and improving the working conditions for faculty who teach off the tenure track.

The roundtable will be followed by a discussion in which members of the audience will be encouraged to ask follow up questions as well as draw from their own experience to help identify some of the many issues involved with organizing contingent faculty today.
