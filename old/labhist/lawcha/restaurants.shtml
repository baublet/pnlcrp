University District

There are many inexpensive and casual locations in the UDistrict, especially along University Ave. Here are a few breakfast spots near Lander Hall followed by a short list of pubs and slightly more upscale but still reasonable restaurants.

Breakfast:

  • Local Point: (in Lander Hall) -- All-you-care-to-eat breakfast for $9.50
  • Bean & Bagel (1410 NE 40th St.) -- coffee, bagels, light fare
  • Portage Bay Café (4130 Roosevelt Way NE)-- amazing waffles
  • Café Solstice (4116 University Way NE)
  • Café on the Ave (4201 University Way NE)

Happy hour pubs:

  • Big Time Brewery (4133 University Way)-- Ten beers on tap and standard pub grub
  • Shultzys Bar and Grill (4114 University Way)--full bar, have your beer and bratwurst too.
  • College Inn Pub (4006 University Way)--basement dive bar
  • Flowers Bar and Restaurant (4247 University Way)

Lunch/ Dinner:

Agua Verde (1303 NE Boat Street) Mexican; good margaritas, overlooking the water http://aguaverde.com/cafe

Ivar's Salmon House (401 NE Northlake Way) Seattle classic on Lake Union, many kinds of salmon and chowder, spectacular views of water, boats, Space Needle. Make reservations but there is also food service in the bar. http://www.ivars.com/locations/salmon-house

Shalimar (4214 University Way) South Asian; quiet with table cloths (rare in the UD) http://eatatshalimar.com

Spring Kitchen (4002 University Way NE) Vietnamese

Xi 'an Noodles (5259 University Way) Known for its handmade noodles (biang biang), perhaps worth the trek up the Ave

Downtown and Belltown

(Recommendations courtesy Michael McCann)

Pike Place Market (1st Ave and Pike St) is the obligatory tourist destination. It’s home to the world’s first Starbucks, Beecher’s cheese (you can watch them make it), and seafood vendors tossing salmon, plus local farmers’ market produce and arts & crafts. There are great food stands and restaurants all up and down the Market, including:

  1. Café Campagne (1600 Post Alley). Excellent French bistro … the heart of France in the heart of Seattle. 206-728-2233. www.cafecampagne.com
  2. Maximilien (81 Pike St). Romantic, with great food and great views. A bit pricey but worth it. 206-682-7271. www.maximilienrestaurant.com
  3. Pike Place Pub &  Brewery (1415 1st Ave). A full selection of Pike ales and pretty decent bar food. Enjoy happy hour from 4:00–6:00pm Monday-Friday. 206-622-6044. www.pikebrewing.com
  4. Lowell’s (1519 Pike Place). Order at the counter on the 1st floor. There are lots of fish options. Then go find a seat on the 3rd floor for great views of Elliott Bay in a casual dining experience. Lunch at Lowell’s is great! Open for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. 7:00am–8:00pm. 206-622-2036. www.eatatlowells.com
  5. Matt’s in the Market (94 Pike St #32). A nice dining experience on the 2nd floor of the Market, with lovely views. The cuisine is local, Northwestern. Reservations are recommended. 206-467-7909. www.mattsinthemarket.com
  6. Oriental Mart (1506 Pike Place Market). First-rate Filipino food. Try the chicken adobo, salmon sinigang, and bulalu. Try to sit near the chef. 206-622-8488. www.yelp.com/biz/oriental-mart-seattle-2
  7. Place Pigalle (81 Pike St). Charming place with great views of Puget Sound and mountains, plus winning cocktails, funky French and Northwest cuisine, generally very good. 206-624-1756. www.placepigalle-seattle.com

    8. Downtown and Belltown (northwest part of downtown)—lots of good options, from inexpensive to pricey.

  8. Le Pichet (1933 1st Ave). A quintessential French bistro located near the Market: mirrored walls, sidewalk seating, and a menu of baguettes, charcuterie, fabulous roasted chicken, salads, and espresso. It’s ideal for lunch or dinner. Superb wine options. Make a reservation. 206-256-1499. www/lepichetseattle.com/home
  9. Bell + Whete (200 Bell St). Nice open space with odes to Middle Ages that seem quite contemporary. Good sampler plates of sausages. 206-538-0180. www.bellandwhete.com
  10. Etta’s (2020 Western Ave). Located just north of the Pike Place Market. Splendid fish restaurant in nice but relaxed room. Also a great destination for weekend brunch. 206-443-6000. tomdouglas.com/index.php?page=ettas
  11. Dahlia Lounge (2001 4th Ave). The breakthrough Tom Douglas. This one serves up New American cuisine, a bit pricey but recent reviews say it is still worth it. 206-682-4142. tomdouglas.com/index.php?page=dahlia-lounge
  12. Palace Kitchen (2030 5th Ave). Pacific Northwest and eclectic Modern American food. Big place, raucous and fun, food is reliably good. 206 448-2001. tomdouglas.com/index.php?page=palace-kitchen
  13. Lola (2000 4th Ave). Greek and Mediterranean food, great tagines, kababs, spreads. Relaxed setting, great bar. Many foodies’ favorite Douglas restaurant. 206-441-1430. tomdouglas.com/index.php?page=lola
  14. The Pink Door (1919 Post Alley). Located just behind the Market, is very good for Italian-American food, and on certain days of the week they have a trapeze artist flying overhead! Reservations recommended. 206-443-3241. www.thepinkdoor.net
  15. Il Bistro (93 A Pike St). South and under the Market. It is old-world Italian, with contemporary touches. A bit pricey, but an elegant place with great food, drinks, wine list. 206-682-3049. www.ilbistro.net
  16. Il Corvo (217 James St). A good bet for lunch for two. Bowls of house-made pasta and sauce. The menu changes daily. Larger groups should expect to sit apart. Located in Pioneer Square (south of the hotel). 11:00am–3:00pm. 206-538-0999. www.ilcorvopasta.com
  17. Il Terrazzo Carmine (411 1st Ave South). Upscale and elegant (so a bit pricey) old-world Italian. Superb food and wines. 206-467-7797. www.ilterrazzocarmine.com
  18. Salumi (309 3rd Ave). World famous salumeria. Offers Italian-style sandwiches with house-made cold cuts for the lunch crowd. The space will not accommodate large groups. 11:00am–3:30pm. 206-621-8772. www.salumicuredmeats.com
  19. Tap House Grill (1506 6th Ave). A crowd-pleaser, with a huge selection of beers on tap, an affordable happy hour, and broad enough menu to satisfy nearly everyone: grill fare, sushi, and vegetarian dishes abound. 11:00am–1:00am. 206-816-3314. taphousegrill.com/index.html
  20. Wild Ginger (1401 3rd Ave). Wild Ginger is a landmark of fusion cuisine, and it continues to produce great food. The green beans are fabulous, as is the duck, seafood, and everything. 4:00–9:00 pm. 206-623-4450. www.wildginger.net
  21. Loulay (600 Union St). Thierry Rautureau brings glitz and glam to downtown’s restaurant scene, yet there is nothing pretentious about it. Sure there’s caviar, foie gras, and French Champagne, but also superlative roast chicken, a burger of distinction and local wines on tap. Most dishes surprisingly affordable. 206-402-4588. www.thechefinthehat.com/loulay-kitchen-seattle.com
  22. Taste at Seattle Art Museum Restaurant (1300 1st Ave). A dozen blocks south of our hotel and offers excellent food from 10:00am–5:00pm. 206-654-3210. www.tastesam.com
  23. Japonessa (1400 1st Ave). Next to Seattle Art Museum, has good sushi. Open from 12:00 noon–11:00pm. 206-971-7979. www.japonessa.com
    Elliott’s Oyster House (1201 Alaska Way). Classy Seattle seafood, with variety of the region’s spectacular oysters. Try Café 56 for cheaper, less formal version. 206-623-4340. www.elliottsoysterhouse.com
  24. The Edgewater Hotel (2411 Alaskan Way, slightly north of downtown). Pacific Northwest cuisine and breathtaking views at its Six Seven Restaurant and Lounge. Open from 7:00am–11:00pm. 206-269-4575. www.edgewaterhotel.com
  25. Lecosho (89 University St). Slightly south of the Market, on the Harbor Steps. Offers American and Pacific Northwest cuisine. Lunch and dinner. 206-623-2101. www.lecosho.com
  26. Gelatiamo (1400 3rd Ave). Delicious gelato and pastries. 206-467-9563.
  27. Spur Gastropub (113 Blanchard). Very good, but spendy, for a pub. They are open from 5:00pm–2:00am. 206-728-6706. www.spurseattle.com
  28. Local 360 (2234 1st Ave). A “Seattle experience,” with sustainable, organic food. Open 8:00am–12:00am. 206-441-9360. www.local360.org
  29. Shiro’s Sushi Restaurant (2401 2nd Ave). Rave reviews. The owner is linked to the “I Dream of Jiro” film. 206-443-9844. www.shiros.com
  30. Fare Start (700) Virginia St). A culinary job training and placement program for homeless and disadvantaged persons, serving very good modern American fare. Note that this restaurant has limited hours of operation. Call or go online for additional information. 206-267-7601. www.farestart.org
  31. Umi Sake House (2230 1st Ave). Hip sushi in the heart of Belltown. 206-374-8717. www.umisakehouse.com
  32. La Vita E Bella (2411 2nd Ave).  Good, simple Italian pasta, seafood, pizzas, salads at very affordable prices. Two rooms – one small, funky, and lively, the other more subdued.  206-441-5123.  www.lavitaebella.us
