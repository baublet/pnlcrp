Class in Session: Teaching Labor History Through Digital Tools

Alex Morrow
UW Tacoma

Abstract

Students learn best when we invite them to be scholars and researchers. My presentation explores some of the techniques and sources I have been using to “flip the classroom” in labor studies courses at UW, Tacoma. These sessions of class utilize digital resources and open-source archives to engage students in various topics in the study of work and labor history. I want to highlight two projects in particular. The first utilizes Social Explorer, the online prescription database tool, which my students have utilized to create an economic report card of their region for the past three years. The second project invites students to create and revise topics in Wikipedia. To date, the majority of entries concerning labor strikes during the Great Depression were created by my students at UW and UW, Tacoma. These kinds of assignments help students transcend the boundaries of the campus and lends gravity to the otherwise abstract concepts we teach in the classroom.
