The Tragedy of the 1971 IRA: British Labour Relations and Modern Conservatism

Declan O'Reilly
University of East Anglia

Abstract

The 1971 industrial relations Act was the most contentious piece of UK labour law in history. It caused considerable industrial unrest in Britain during the less than two years of its operation and was repealed by the Wilson Labour government in 1974. However, the 1971 IRA was the first genuine attempt at comprehensive modern labour reform in Britain, this paper seeks to contextualise it with Britain changing political landscape of the 1970s and wider European events at the same time.
