In large part this was due to the success of the Washington Commonwealth Federation and the Washington Pension Union. The WCF functioned as a left-wing caucus of the state Democratic Party, working to set legislative priorities and to get progressive candidates nominated and elected on the Democratic ticket. In some cases, notably the election of Hugh DeLacy to the U.S. Congress in 1944, these Democrats were secretly members of the Communist Party. The WPU was a mass organization that lobbied for higher old age insurance for the elderly. At their peak in the late 1930s and early 1940s, each organization boasted tens of thousands of members statewide and the WCF largely controlled the state Democratic Party apparatus.
