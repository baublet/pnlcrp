Inspired by the Bolshevik Revolution in October 1917 and energized by the Seattle General Strike, in 1919 the left-wing of the state Socialist Party broke away to form a branch of the Communist Party of America (CPA). A rival party, the Communist Labor Party, was founded at the same time by former members of foreign-language sections of the Socialist Party. In Washington, as elsewhere throughout the country, the two factions merged in 1921 to become the Communist Party USA.
Seattle became the headquarters of CPUSA District 12, encompassing Washington, Oregon and Idaho.
