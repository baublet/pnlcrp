Communists also played a critical role in the revitalization of organized labor in the Pacific Northwest in the 1930s and 1940s. Party members could be found in nearly every union, but were especially prominent in the maritime, timber, and canning industries. Party unionists helped lead some of the major strikes of the period, including the 1934 waterfront workers strike, the 1935 timber workers strike, and the 1936 Seattle Newspaper Guild strike. They were also among the most outspoken champions of the Congress of Industrial Organizations, working to push many AFL locals into the new CIO in the late 1930s.
