The wage dispute led to a strike by the Metal Trades Council, the bargaining unit for unionized Seattle shipyard workers, which began on January 21, 1919. Tacoma shipyard workers followed suit.
The Central Labor Council believed that the struggle in the shipyards was the first salvo in a massive postwar open shop drive. Success there was pivotal to the strength of all organized labor in Seattle, and indeed throughout the nation.
