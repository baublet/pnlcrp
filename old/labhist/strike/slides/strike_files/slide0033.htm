The Project also includes an interview with Earl George, an African American longshoreman who participated in the strike. He would later become an important leader in the Washington State Communist Party and the first black president of an ILWU local - Seattle’s local 9.
George was one of only a few members of Seattle’s African American population to belong to a union in 1919. The interracial International Longshoremen’s Association was the exception in a nearly all-white Seattle labor movement. 
 “Nothing moved but the tide.”
-Earl George, 1977
