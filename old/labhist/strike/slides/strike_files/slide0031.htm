1920 Labor Day float sponsored by Deep Sea Salvage Company, a prominent example of labor capitalism emerging from Seattle’s post-strike labor movement.
Many of Seattle’s worker cooperatives and labor-owned businesses predated the general strike and continued to thrive after the strike ended and into the early 1920s. The impetus the strike gave to “consumer organizing” on the part of the labor movement is one of the most important legacies of February 1919. 
Inside a branch store of the Seattle Consumer’s Cooperative. This store distributed 1,000 free loaves of bread in a single day during the general strike.
