More than 20,000 people work at the University of Washington, making UW one of the largest employers in the state. They include faculty and graduate student employees who teach and conduct research on the three campuses; nurses, doctors, and technicians who staff the two university hospitals;  professional and clerical staff who make the departments, programs, libraries, labs, and offices work;  maintenance and custodial workers who keep the buildings clean and safe; and many, many more doing hundreds of different kinds of jobs, all essential to the mission of the university.

 

Almost half of UW’s employees belong to unions or professional organizations which bargain collectively or otherwise represent their members interests. Twelve different unions and professional organizations are active on campus. Some have long histories, others are part of the new surge of unionism that has been reshaping many universities in the last few years. In 2002 Washington state legislature passed  laws establishing collective bargaining rights for almost all university employees. These measures insure that unions are going to be an even more important part of the University of Washington in the years to come.

 

Welcome to the The Workers and Unions of UW Project, a resource for learning more about the University of Washington and the people who make it work. Sponsored by the Harry Bridges Center for Labor Studies, This website  provides information about the work experiences of employees, about the unions and associations that represent them, and about issues and events affecting academic workers. The project began in conjunction with History 450 (Class and Labor in American History) taught by Professor James Gregory Spring quarter 2002 and many of the reports found here were written by members of that class. Brian Grijalva designed the web site. It is a work in progress. We will be adding to it in the months ahead.

Unions of UW

Seven AFL-CIO unions have members on campus. We have reports on their missions, leadership, and also their history:

  • Washington Federation of State Employees Local 1488 represents close to 1,800 workers in dozens of job categories. WFSE helped pioneer collective bargaining rights for state employees in Washington.
  • SEIU Local 925 Classified Staff Association is the largest campus union, representing nearly 5,000 workers including most of the clerical staff.
  • Graduate Student Employee Action Coalition/UAW is the newest campus union representing more than 2,000 teaching assistants, research assistants, tutors, and graders.
  • SEIU 1199NW represents more than 1,000 nurses and staff at Harborview Hospital.
  • Graphic Communications International Union, Local 767M represents employees in the UW print shop. It is the oldest of the campus unions.
  • United Food and Commercial Workers Union Local 1001 represents department of laboratory medicine employees.
  • Inlandboatmen's Union of the Pacific, ILWU represents the crew of the Puget Sound/Thompson research vessel.

 

Professional Associations of UW

These organizations are not AFL-CIO affiliates and most do not have collective bargaining agreements with the University.

  • American Association of University Professors represents several hundred faculty members. It has had an active chapter at UW since 1918.
  • Association of Librarians of University of Washington has represented the Universities librarians since the 1960s
  • Professional Staff Organization speaks for the more than 4,000 professional staff on the three campuses
  • Washington State Nurses Association represents registered nurses at University Hospital. It has a collective bargaining agreement with UW.

·        Police Officers Association represents the campus police officers. It has a collective bargaining agreement with UW.
